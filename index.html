The School Air Mission

“For Climate Awareness and Clean Air in All Schools”

A Non-Profit collaborative effort by, and for middle and high school students worldwide.

Next

Why School Air?

Student research has determined that existing Meteorological stations, in our pilot city, Barcelona, are not providing real time air quality status for schools in their immediate area. Sensors are primarily in green public parks and on high spots of the city such as radio towers and at the top of hills. It was determined that there is an urgent need for schools to monitor the air quality of their particular locale – so that they may take protective actions as necessary.

To ensure the health of our young students and provide an opportunity for a meaningful technical project, Alella Green Tech is sponsoring the schoolAIR project: students will use the latest microcontrollers and various different types of sensors, to build an air quality and weather station from scratch. This will give students an excellent foundation in environmental science, data science, electronics and programming. Air & Weather stations will be installed at each participating school.

Project Components

Our team has meticulously researched and selected components to create a system that is both powerful and economical. By using modern, high-quality, yet affordable microcontrollers and sensors, we've designed a modular and extensible station. The overall cost has been kept remarkably low, making this vital technology accessible and affordable for most schools, maximizing the benefits for students everywhere.

Students of Different Ages Working Together

Over June and July of 2025, students from representative schools started on the project by researching the need for monitoring air quality in schools - using Barcelona as the first location - and designing the overall system. Our first team of young data science pioneers was made up of students of ages 12 through 24 – all collaborating to build the system together. We found that the older students enjoyed explaining the thornier concepts to the younger ones and the team pulled through very well together and completed the challenging project.

A Global Project with a Big Vision

Global School Participation

Complete air quality and weather station kits, along with all the necessary open-source software and documentation, will be made available to participating schools. The schoolAir project will also provide the core infrastructure, including server and database access, so that schools can easily build a system that facilitates monitoring and trend analysis. Extensive climate and air quality data will be downloadable from the central schoolAIR.org website.

The schoolAIR.org website will allow schools to easily join the project and request the necessary materials and plans. Participating schools will be able to visit the parameter information page for their campus, while all public data will be anonymized to protect privacy. Global data across all schools or selected areas will be shown as aggregated trends only. The idea is to give everyone the tools to identify the general areas where air quality improvement is necessary to keep our children healthy and performing at their best.

Beyond Monitoring

Beyond monitoring the outside area for pollutants and the inside areas for the right composition of the air children breathe, we are investigating partnerships with corporate entities that provide products that improve air quality in closed spaces. It is a well known fact that an excess of CO2 in a closed room causes lower cognitive performance and focus. As schools identify zones where air quality can be improved, the schoolAIR.org project will seek to provide guidelines for improvement as well as identifying companies and products that specifically improve air quality – but simply being prompted to open a window would be a great first step forwards!

A growing schoolAIR Community

The schoolAIR.org team is proud that our first pioneering students have already put together the system and will be working as ambassadors and technical liaison in the first pilot schools. As more schools take up the project, a community of technically able and very motivated students will form.

The non-profit schoolAIR.org project will go live at its first school in September. Interested schools and individuals can request more information at the project website or write to info@schoolAIR.org
